## 3.**We are building a second divider. We want $V_2$ to be one fifth of $V_{in}$. If $R_1$ is $9.50\ohm$ , what resistance should we choose for $R_2$? $(1.00MΩ = 1.00*106\ohm)$.  Show formulas used:**

$$V_2 = \frac{1}{5}V_{in} \quad | \quad R_1 = 9.50\ohm$$
1. Step 1 
$$V_2 = \frac{V_{in}}{R_1 + R_2}R_2$$
2. Step 2
$$\rightarrow \frac{V_{in}}{5} = \frac{V_{in}}{9.50\ohm + R_2}R_2$$
3. Step 3
$$\rightarrow V_{in} = \frac{5V_{in}}{9.50\ohm + R_2}R_2$$
4. Step 4
$$\frac{V_{in}}{R_2} = \frac{5V_{in}}{9.50\ohm + R_2}$$
5. Step 5
$$V_{in}(9.50\ohm + R_2) = 5V_{in}R_2$$
6. Step 6
$$\rightarrow V_{in}9.50\ohm + V_{in}R_2 = 5V_{in}R_2$$
7. Step 7
$$\rightarrow V_{in}9.50\ohm = 4V_{in}R_2$$
8. Step 8
$$R_2 = \frac{V_{in}9.50\ohm}{4V_{in}}$$
9. Assuming $V_{in} = 10V$
$$\rightarrow R_2 = \frac{V_{in}9.50\ohm}{4V_{in}} = \frac{10V * 9.50\ohm}{4*10V} = 2.375\ohm$$
